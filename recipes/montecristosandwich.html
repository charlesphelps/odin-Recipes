Monte Cristo Sandwich Recipe

Behind the dish

Though it has been found in American cookbooks since the 1930's, the Monte Cristo found its name and popularity at the Blue Bayou Restaurant in Disneyland during the 1960's. The name is a reference to the famous novel The Count of Monte Cristo.

Ingrediants

  • Three slices of white bread
  • A quater pound of ham
  • A half cup of gouda
  • Two tablespoons of Mayonnaise (optional)
  • One large egg
  • Two tablespoons of milk
  • One teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper
  • Timer
  • Two tablespoons of butter
  • A plate
  • A bowl
  • A fork
  • A skillet

Directions

  1. Grate gouda on top of two slices of bread and place a slice of ham over each
  2. Spread a thin coat of mayonnaise on both sides of the third slice
  3. Sandwich this slice between the two slices of bread with gouda and ham so that a plane side of bread is on top and bottom afterwards
  4. Compress the sandwich with a plate for several seconds
  5. Whisk the egg, milk, paprika, and a pinch of salt and pepper together in a bowl
  6. Melt butter on a skillet using medium heat
  7. Soak the sandich on both sides in the egg mixture until completely covered
  8. Place on skillet, and let both sides cook for roughly three and a half minutes (until golden brown)
  9. Press the sides of the sandwich onto the skillet to cook for 15 seconds per side
