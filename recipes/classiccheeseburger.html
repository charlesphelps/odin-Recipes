Classic Cheeseburger Recipe

Behind the dish

The cheeseburger found its start in 19th century America, where cattle ranching's expansion into the Great Plains provided ample access to beef in the American Diet. The Hamburger was an affordable result of this, and by the 1920's, a piece of cheese was placed on top of the patty, and the cheeseburger was born.

Ingrediants

  • Ground beef (preferably 20% fat)
  • Butter
  • Hamburger bun(s)
  • Sliced cheese
  • Condiments
  • Lettuce
  • Tomatoe
  • Onion
  • Pickle (optional)
  • Skillet or pan
  • Spatula
  • Wax paper
  • Warm plate

Directions

  1. Turn on heat source to highest setting (placing pan or skillet on top)
  2. Take quarter pound of ground beef and work into a ball (one ball per burger)
  3. Fold or cut wax paper into squares roughly the size of the finsihed burger
  4. Once "ripping hot" place slab of butter on top, coating top of pan or skillet
  5. Place beef balls on skillet or pan, spaced to allow room between once smashed into patties
  6. Place wax paper squarely over each ball, and press down hard (smash) with the spatula squarely over the wax paper
  7. Continue step above until each and every ball is evenly smashed to into a patty (the thinner the crispier)
  8. Let cook for about a minute, then flip
  9. Immediately place sliced cheese on each patty
  10. After another minute, plate each patty, and turn off the stove
  11. Let the patties sit for 5 minutes
  12. During this time, slice onions, tomatoes, lettuce and pickles
  13. Put condiment on flat side of bun
  14. Place patty on flat side bun, and dress with two rings of onion, a layer of lettuce, a slice of tomatoe, and two slices of pickle
  15. Place the rounded top bun on top, and flip burger over so the flat side is on top
