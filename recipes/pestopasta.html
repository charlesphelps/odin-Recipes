Pesto Pasta Recipe

Behind the dish

Pesto pastas' roots date back to Ancient Rome (where its initial recipe was called Moretum). Through its continuos evolution, the pasta has evolved to traditionally use mandilli de s√¶a, trofie, or trenette pastas, and includes boiled potatoes and string beans. The sauce is made from pine nuts, basil, salt, garlic, oilve oil, and parmesan-reggiano or a similar substitute. Given its global popularity, various ingrediants have found their way into "pesto" recipes. These may include a variety of nuts in lieu of pine nuts, herbs added to the basil such as mint, kale, dill, and coriander, and a variety of hard cheeses. As is common however, in Italy, the traditional recipe rules.

Ingrediants

  • Store bought pasta
  • Pesto pasta sauce
  • Parmesan or a similar hard cheese
  • Cherry tomatoes (optional)
  • Basil leaf (optional)
  • Cooking pliers
  • Water
  • A pot
  • Timer
  • A warmed plate(s)
  • A Fork

Directions

  1. Decide on desired serving size, and remove appropriate amount of pasta
  2. Place pot on stove, and fill with water so pasta will be well submerged
  3. Bring water to a rolling boil
  4. Once boiling, place pasta inside the pot
  5. Let cook for amount of time listed on pasta box (typically 8-12 minutes)
  6. Once ready, remove pasta from water in pot, and turn off the stove
  7. Mix in pesto sauce with pasta while in the pot, stirring while adding. It should look similar to the consistency seen in the image above.
  8. (Optional) Slice one or two cherry tomatoes per person and stir into pasta
  9. Twirl pasta around cooking pliers, and place on plate, like above
  10. (Optional) Place basil leaf on top of nest
  11. (Optional) Once all plates are served, stick remaining tomatoes in pot into the nests (aiming for 2-4 per person)
